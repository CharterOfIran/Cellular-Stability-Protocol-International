This protocol is designed to establish "Cellular Security" without the need for weaponry, relying instead on **Collective Vigilance**. Our objective is to "See" without necessarily being "Seen" or engaging in "Conflict."

---

## 1. Structure of Observation Posts

* **Vertical Observation (Rooftop):** Stationing at the highest point of the building to oversee the end of the alley and surrounding streets. 
    * **Duty:** Identifying suspicious vehicle movements or gatherings of unknown forces.
* **Horizontal Observation (Entry):** Stationing behind the main entrance or lobby (without exiting the building). 
    * **Duty:** Monitoring pedestrian traffic and ensuring the physical integrity of entry points.

---

## 2. Identification Protocol (Shibboleth)

In the darkness of night, distinguishing a neighbor from a stranger is vital:

* **Vocal Code (Shibboleth):** Agree upon a simple, everyday word (e.g., "Rain") among neighbors. If someone approaches, the guard asks: "Who is it?" and the person must respond with the code.
* **Light Signals:** Use three short flashlight flashes pointed at the ground (not at the person's face) to confirm identity from a distance.

---

## 3. Alert System and Response (Chain of Command)

* **The Golden Rule:** The guard's duty is **NOT** "Physical Confrontation." Upon detecting danger, the guard must activate the alert system to wake the entire "Neighborhood Cell."
* **Collective Pressure:** Simultaneously turning on building lights or neighbors appearing on balconies is the most powerful deterrent against security threats.
* **Alert Methods:** Use whistles, air horns, or rhythmic banging on metal objects (pots/spoons) to trigger an immediate neighborhood-wide alarm.

---

## 4. Essential Monitoring Equipment

* **Radio:** To monitor official news and city-wide status updates.
* **Flashlight:** Only for momentary signaling; keeping it on continuously reveals your position.
* **Noise Makers:** Whistles or any object capable of creating a loud, sudden sound to alert the community.
