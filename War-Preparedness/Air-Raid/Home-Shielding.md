This protocol is engineered to minimize internal casualties caused by blast overpressure and structural vibrations. The goal is to transform your living space into a **"Civilian Fortress"** before a crisis occurs.

---

## 1. Designating and Equipping the "Safe Spot"
Identify a primary anchor point within your residence:
* **Location Criteria:** Windowless interior rooms, central hallways (shielded from the building's facade), or reinforced stairwell landings.
* **The Two-Wall Rule:** Your safe spot should ideally have at least two structural walls between you and the exterior environment to act as a **Structural Buffer**.
* **Essential Gear:** Store mats, blankets, and pillows here. Pillows are vital for head protection during the moment of impact.

## 2. Mitigation of Glass Hazards (Primary Trauma Source)
A blast wave transforms windows into high-velocity shrapnel.
* **Cross-Taping (X-Pattern):** Apply heavy-duty adhesive tape in a large "X" across all glass panes. While this won't prevent breaking, it significantly reduces the fragmentation and scattering of micro-shards.
* **Blast Curtains:** Keep thick, heavy curtains drawn at all times. They act as a "catch-net," decelerating flying glass before it enters the room's interior.
* **Sill Clearance:** Remove all potential projectiles (vases, frames, decor) from window sills immediately.

## 3. Securing Heavy Interior Assets
* **Bedroom Safety:** Relocate heavy paintings, mirrors, or bookshelves away from the area directly above beds.
* **Anchor Points:** If possible, secure tall cabinets and bookcases to the walls using simple metal brackets to prevent "tip-over" injuries during seismic-like vibrations.
* **Lighting Fixtures:** Replace heavy, ornate chandeliers in high-traffic areas with lightweight, secure lighting to eliminate overhead risks.

## 4. Critical Utility Management
* **Main Shut-off Awareness:** Ensure every household member knows the exact location of the **Main Gas Valve** and **Circuit Breaker**. Practice rapid deactivation in total darkness.
* **Leak Prevention:** Verify that all gas connections (heaters, stoves) are secured with flexible, high-pressure hoses. Minor vibrations can trigger secondary gas leaks and explosions.

## 5. The "Stay-Bin" (Emergency Cache)
Pre-position these items in your designated Safe Spot to avoid searching for them during a siren:
* **Hydration & Hygiene:** At least 2 gallons of water (for drinking and wound irrigation).
* **Illumination:** High-lumen flashlights with extra batteries.
* **Communication:** A battery-powered radio for offline updates.
* **Basic Trauma Kit:** Gauze, disinfectants, and essential medications.
